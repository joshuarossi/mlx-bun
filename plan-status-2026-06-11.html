01 the headline result — resolved 2026-06-11

The decode gap was a phantom — root-caused and fixed.

+4.6% 12B decode @600 vs mlx-lm · post-fix, paired (25.1 vs 24.0) and parity @8k (23.8 vs 23.9) — the −11% → −4.5% "context-scaling gap" is gone.

The per-step wall-time split (both stacks, @600 and @8k) found it in one session. Root cause 1: the first decode step after a long prefill pays a one-shot allocator-reclaim stall — 807 ms after an 8k prefill — clearing the prefill's transient buffers. mlx-lm sidesteps it with mx.clear_cache() after every prefill chunk; we never called it. Root cause 2: mlx-lm stops its prompt clock at the first yielded token, so the boundary stall lands in python's prefill time — while we billed it to decode. The "gap" was largely a different clock measuring a real cost.

The fix (all reference-faithful, in generate.ts): clear_cache after each prefill chunk + after token 0 + every 256 tokens, and the same clock-swap accounting. 118/118 tests; bit-exact parity untouched. Remaining honest negative: e4b still trails −5% — its 17 ms steps expose our ~2–4 ms serial per-step graph-build (bun:ffi op calls) proportionally harder. Next lever: mlx_compile (Phase 7).
